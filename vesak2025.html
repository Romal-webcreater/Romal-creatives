About Vesak

Vesak, also known as Buddha Day, is the most sacred festival in the Buddhist calendar. It celebrates the birth, enlightenment (nirvana), and passing away (parinirvana) of Gautama Buddha. The day is marked by religious activities, decorations, almsgiving, and acts of compassion. People light Vesak lanterns, create artistic pandals, and participate in dana (giving) to honor the Buddha's teachings.

Celebration in Sri Lanka

In Sri Lanka, Vesak is a national holiday celebrated with great devotion and joy. Streets are illuminated with colorful lanterns (vesak koodu), and massive pandals (thoran) tell Jataka stories. Many engage in sil programs, meditate, and offer free food and drinks (dansal) to the public. It's a time for spiritual reflection and spreading kindness to all living beings.
